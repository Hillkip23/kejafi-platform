Key Findings

Four empirical and simulation-based conclusions

01Population Growth Drives Rents
R² = 0.73 (p < 0.01) — population growth explains 73% of long-run rent variation. Austin (13.9%) leads all study markets. The OU framework successfully captures this dynamic.
02Elasticity Determines Volatility
San Francisco (ε = 0.08) shows 15.2% rent volatility vs. Charlotte's 8.5%. Inelastic markets exhibit 40–50% higher volatility, validating the OU σ-adjustment model.
03Tokenisation is Economically Viable
IRRs of 12.3% (FINE5) and 14.1% (FINE6) exceed the 5% risk-free rate. Sharpe ratio of 0.77 is competitive with broad equity indices. Gas cost $145 vs. $685K–$1.25M assets.
04Diversification Reduces VaR by 32%
Cross-metro allocation reduces portfolio VaR from single-asset levels by 32%, validating geographic diversification as a core risk management tool in tokenised real estate.
Conclusion
Kejafi provides a replicable template for the next generation of RWA tokenisation platforms
Three-stage platform integrates OU stochastic modelling, live Uniswap V3 deployment, and institutional-grade portfolio analytics in a unified, open-source framework.
Population growth (R² = 0.73) and supply elasticity are the two dominant drivers of urban rental market risk — directly relevant to Urban Economics.
The CLARITY Act, Ondo Finance, Deloitte's $1.4T projection, and Canada's CSA guidance all signal that mainstream RWA tokenisation is imminent.
Geographic diversification reduces portfolio VaR by 32% — a quantitative argument for cross-metro tokenised real estate allocation.
